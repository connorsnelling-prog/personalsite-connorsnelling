My EDC Setup

There are three things I carry every day without thinking about it.

The first is a Victorinox Classic SD. It's the smallest Swiss Army knife they make — 58mm, six tools, fits on a keychain. The scissors are the most useful thing I own. People think of Swiss Army knives as survival gear. This one is office gear. I use the scissors to open packages, the blade to split cable ties, the file to deal with hangnails in meetings. The screwdriver has never been used. I keep it anyway.

The second is a Muji card case. Matte, slim, holds about ten cards. I switched to this from a wallet a while back and I'm not going back. I don't carry cash. I don't need receipts. I need my bank card, my travel card, and two forms of ID. Everything else is noise.

The third is a Muji Planted Tree Notebook in A7. It's about the size of a playing card. I use it to write things down when I don't want to look at a screen — a thought mid-walk, a word I want to look up later, a thing I don't want to forget. I go through maybe three a year. The paper is good. The size is the point.

The whole setup weighs almost nothing. That's also the point.
